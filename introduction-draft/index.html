後來在 OpenNet 任職時，我把它從 0 到 1 完整搭建起來，做出一套前後端都能用的完整流程，用它所交付的 feature 比團隊的估時快 42%、AI 生成的程式碼採用率超過 70%，同時 feature 開發完後，團隊會有一套與 codebase 高度相關的 Tech Design Document，可用於打造團隊的 RAG knowledge base、並能回溯 code 當初的抉擇脈絡。Later at OpenNet, I built it out from 0 to 1 into a complete process usable by both front-end and back-end. Maxora's team is unusual — most members came from Microsoft and were researchers; great at research, but relatively light on software-process discipline. I filled the gaps in their existing workflow and introduced AI-driven development. The result: rework dropped 41% and feature delivery time shrank 33%.

4TikTok｜把 AI 落地、把制度留下TikTok ｜ Landing AI, Leaving the System Behind

另一方面，我是直播團隊對接 AI 團隊的窗口——前面提到的那套 AI 驅動開發流程，最早就是在這裡討論、試錯並落地的。我負責收集團隊使用 AI IDE（Trae）的回饋，再跟 AI 團隊一起討論、迭代，最終導入一套適合我們直播團隊的 AI 驅動開發流程，讓 feature 的 bug 產生率降了 33%、feature 估時降了 24%。On the other side, I was the live team's liaison to the AI team — the AI-driven process I mentioned was first debated, trialed and landed right here. I gathered the team's feedback on using an AI IDE (Trae), iterated with the AI team, and rolled out a process fitted to our live-streaming team, cutting feature bug rate by 33% and feature estimates by 24%.

我還和 AI 團隊、QA 團隊一起做了一套線上 issue 自動分流系統：當 QA 開出線上問題，我們會把使用者操作過程的所有前端事件和後端事件撈出來，交給 LLM 判讀，自動決定這張 ticket 該轉給客戶端還是後端、並說明問題可能出在哪。這等於做到了跨 repo、跨 log database 的初步資料統整，最後讓線上問題的修復時間縮短了 22%。I also built, with the AI and QA teams, an automatic triage system for production issues: when QA files a live issue, we pull all the front-end and back-end events from the user's session, hand them to an LLM, and it automatically decides whether the ticket goes to client or back-end and explains where the problem likely is. That amounted to a first cut at consolidating data across repos and log databases, and it shortened production-issue fix time by 22%.

我也曾因為自己的疏失導致 Live Revenue Matrix 下降，因此主持了一場 RCA meeting。最終催生出一套「當 feature 跨多個團隊時該有的協作流程」，並讓 AI 團隊立項了一個內部系統：它包含一道跨團隊的 QA 閘門、一個在開發前期就啟動的跨團隊討論機制，以及一套用 LLM 自動把異動指標關聯到可疑 feature 的內部系統。I also once caused a drop in the Live Revenue Matrix through my own oversight, so I ran an RCA meeting. It ultimately produced a "collaboration process for when a feature spans multiple teams," and prompted the AI team to charter an internal system: a cross-team QA gate, a cross-team discussion mechanism kicked off early in development, and a system that uses an LLM to automatically correlate moved metrics back to the suspect feature.

6我的代表作：一套每天在跑的 agentic AI 平台My Signature Work: An Agentic AI Platform That Runs Daily

同時我在工作之外，獨力從 0 到 1 打造並維運一套個人化 agentic AI 平台。一開始的動機很單純：我想充實自己在 AI agent 應用上的實戰知識，同時我也真的需要一套工具，能自動跨多個社群平台收集資料、經過一連串處理去蕪存菁，最後建成一個對 AI agent 友善、可搜尋的 RAG 知識庫。Outside of work, I single-handedly built and operate a personal agentic AI platform from 0 to 1. The motivation was simple: I wanted hands-on depth in AI-agent applications, and I genuinely needed a tool that could automatically gather data across multiple social platforms, run it through a pipeline that separates signal from noise, and build an agent-friendly, searchable RAG knowledge base.

這套平台現在有 33 個模組、串接了 3 家廠商的 LLM。它每天會自動從 3 個社群平台——Twitter、小紅書、YouTube——以及多家知名 AI 公司的技術部落格和官網，穩定爬進 200 篇以上的文章和影片（目前已穩定運行 1 個半月），爬下的內容再經過一條完整的 pipeline：媒體解析、分類評分、內容增強、到專家審查，最後沉澱成可搜尋的知識庫。The platform now has 33 modules and integrates LLMs from 3 vendors. Every day it automatically pulls 200+ articles and videos from 3 social platforms — Twitter, RED (Xiaohongshu) and YouTube — plus the tech blogs and sites of several well-known AI companies (it's run stably for 1.5 months). The captured content then flows through a full pipeline — media parsing, classification & scoring, content enrichment, and expert review — and settles into a searchable knowledge base.

過程中我也用 LLM 的 A/B test，在只損失 3% 解讀表現的前提下，把運行成本減少 83%。這整件事對我最大的意義，是它證明我真的能把一個模糊的 AI 想法，落地成可量測、可長期維運的系統。而我最深的體會是：要讓 AI 變成每天能用的東西，難的從來不是模型本身，而是它周邊的 pipeline、錯誤處理和成本控制——也就是把 AI「工程化」的那段功夫。Along the way I used LLM A/B testing to cut running cost by 83% while losing only 3% of interpretation quality. What this means most to me is that it proves I can take a fuzzy AI idea and land it as a measurable, long-running system. My deepest takeaway: making AI an everyday tool — the hard part was never the model itself, but the surrounding pipeline, error handling and cost control — the work of "engineering" AI.
